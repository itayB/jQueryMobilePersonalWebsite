GPS Signals Simulation In Urban Canyons

Team Members: Eran Friedman and Itay Bittan.

Advisors: Prof. Matya Katz, Dr. Paz Carmi and Dr. Boaz Ben-Moshe.

Background: GPS satellites transmit signals to earth, containing information such as their time synchronization and location. The receiver extracts this information and can calculate its distance from the satellites. Knowing the distances from four different satellites, the receiver can compute its 3D location on earth by performing spheres intersection.

The Problem: When a GPS receiver is located among buildings, it may be that the signal doesn‘t reach the receiver directly, but it is reflected off the buildings. This phenomenon increases the calculated distance between the satellite and the receiver, and leads to an error in the receiver location.

Motivation: Urban navigation, Black box, Autonomous driving, etc.

Experiment in the Burssa, Ramat-Gan
Blue Path- The real path.
Red Path- As measured by GPS receiver.

The Project: The purpose of this project is to simulate the multipath behavior of GPS signals in urban canyons: given a 3D map of buildings in preprocessing phase, and given a receiver and a satellite locations in query time, we want:
   • to check the existence of LOS (line of sight) between them.
   • to calculate the signals multipath (i.e., the different legal paths in which the signals can reach the receiver).
